Fettucine Alfredo



Description

Fettuccine Alfredo is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich and creamy sauce. Fettuccine Alfredo is a decadent and satisfying pasta dish that's loved by any for its creamy, comforting flavors. It can be enjoyed as a stand-alone meal or paired with a side salad or garlic bread.

Fettuccine Alfredo has a relatively short but intriguing history that is tied to a specific Italian restaurant and its founder. The dish is believed to have been created in the early 20th century and is known for its rich and creamy sauce. It's important to note that while Fettuccine Alfredo is considered a classic Italian-American dish, it is not a traditional Italian recipe in the same sense as many other Italian pasta dishes. Instead, it's a creation of a sepcific restaurant and chef, Alfredo di Lelio, and has become part of Italian-American culinary heritage.


Ingredients

  • Fettuccine Pasta
  • Butter
  • Heavy Cream
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. In a large pot, boil and drain fettuccine noodles according to the package instructions(until al dente)
  2. While the noodles are cooking, add 2 tb butter to a large skillet and medium heat, and let melt. Add the minced garlic and saute for 1-2 minutes. Add heavy cream and bring it to a simmer. Lastly, add 1 cup parmesan Cheese and mix well.
  3. Add pasta to the cream mixture and stir until covered. Top with salt and pepper and more parmesan cheese.
